Detailed Shift

Definition screen

Field descriptions

Detailed Shift

Detailed shift is used in case there are many times of scanning card in a shift or there is a break at the middle of shift.

For example:
Regular workday begins at 8:00 and ends at 17:00 and there is an-hour break for lunch. In this case, you have to define 2 detailed shifts for this shift.

(Time In/Out) Next Day

This option is selected in case a shift begins on one day and ends on the next day; or a detail of this shift is in the next day.
When getting timekeeping data, the system will recognize that the time in/out on one day of a detailed shift with check on the "Next Day" option will be timekeeping data of the previous day.
In case of time in/out adjusting, the "Next Day" option is checked at "Employee Time In/Out Adjusting" function because the system cannot edit the manually input information. At that time, the date input is the previous date and check on "Next Day" option.

Late In/ Early Out

If the actual late in minutes is less than "Late In" minutes defined in this field, the system will consider as not late in and vice versa.
If the actual early out minutes is less than "Early Out" minutes defined in this field, the system will consider as not early out and vice versa.

Note:
Late in/early out minutes is only used to determine if an employee is late in/early out or not. The working hours will be calculated by actual time (minus the late in/early out minutes).

Added Minutes

This field is used for shifts with the duration shorter than actual one so that these shifts will recorded with full hours.

For example:
As usual, the number of working hours on Saturday is 4. Once all employees are allowed 1-hour early out. The Saturday shift (detailed shift) must be defined from 7am to 11am with the "Added Minutes" as 60.

Workday

Workday sign of detailed shift; extracted from Workday Sign List. If the shift code category is 2 - Overtime, only the workday signs with category as "Overtime" (*) will be shown. If the shift code category is 1 - Regular, the workday signs with category as "Overtime" (*) will not be shown.

(*) The fact that a workday sign is overtime or not depends on "Workday Type" of that workday sign (refer to "Category" option in Workday Type List).
